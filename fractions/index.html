Video Playlist

Four fun fraction lessons

Each lesson focuses on one big idea so students can watch, pause, rewind, and work through examples one step at a time.

Video 1

What is a Fraction?

Learn how fractions split a whole or set and what is a numerator and denominator.

Play What Is a Fraction?
  • Fractions describe equal parts of one whole.
  • The numerator counts the parts used or shaded.
  • The denominator tells how many equal parts make the whole.
Video 2

How to Add Fractions

Learn when you can add right away and when you first need to rewrite fractions using a common denominator.

Play How to Add Fractions <
  • If the denominators match, add the numerators.
  • If they do not match, find a common denominator first.
  • Simplify your final answer when you can.
Video 3

How to Subtract Fractions

Practice taking away parts of a whole while keeping track of common denominators and simplified answers.

Play How to Subtract Fractions
  • Rewrite with common denominators before subtracting.
  • Subtract the top numbers and keep the denominator.
  • Check if the result can be simplified.
Video 4

How to Multiply Fractions

See the shortcut: multiply across the top, multiply across the bottom, and then reduce the answer if possible.

Play How to Multiply Fractions
  • Multiply numerator by numerator.
  • Multiply denominator by denominator.
  • Simplify the product if there is a smaller equivalent fraction.

Fraction Toolkit

Quick reminders before you press play

These are the words and moves that show up again and again in the videos. A fast review here makes the lessons easier to follow.

Top number

Numerator

The numerator tells how many equal parts you have, use, shade, or count.

3/8 The 3 is the numerator.

Bottom number

Denominator

The denominator tells how many equal parts make one whole.

3/8 The 8 is the denominator.

Team move

Common Denominator

To add or subtract fractions, you often rewrite them so the bottom numbers match.

1/2 = 2/4 Now it matches 1/4.

Make it smaller

Simplify

A simplified fraction uses smaller numbers but still shows the same amount.

4/8 = 1/2 Same value, simpler form.

Math Moves

Fast reminders

Pause the video after each example, solve one step on your own, and then press play. That is usually enough to turn "I kind of get it" into "I can do this."
  1. Read the denominator first so you know the size of the parts.
  2. For addition and subtraction, match the denominators before combining parts.
  3. For multiplication, multiply across and simplify at the end.
